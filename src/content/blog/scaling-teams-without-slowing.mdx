# Scaling Teams Without Slowing Down

Draft scaffold — outline subject to change.

## Premise
- Growth usually adds coordination tax and slows delivery.
- The goal is to increase throughput without accumulating ceremony, drag, or risk.

## Operating Principles
- Small, shippable slices with measurable outcomes.
- Clear interfaces and ownership; fewer handoffs.
- Standardize where it compounds; allow variation where it’s leverage.

## Practices To Cover
- Team topology and service boundaries.
- Guardrails over gates (pre-prod checks, golden metrics, SLO-driven deploys).
- Pragmatic platform: paved roads and templates that remove accidental complexity.
- Observability defaults: RED + tracing + budgets per surface.
- Cost visibility as a first-class feedback loop.

## Anti-patterns
- Process accretion as a response to incidents.
- Tool sprawl without ownership.
- “One-size-fits-all” platforms that create local drag.

## Outcomes
- Faster cycle time with fewer regressions.
- Predictable reliability and spend as the org scales.
